üöÄ Getting Started

How do I get started with the trading bot?
  1. Sign up for an account if you haven't already
  2. Configure your settings - Go to Settings page and review/update your trading parameters
  3. Start in paper trading mode - This is enabled by default and lets you test without risking real money
  4. Connect Coinbase API (optional) - Add your API keys in Settings for real market data
  5. Start the bot - Click the "Start" button on the Overview page
  6. Monitor performance - Watch the dashboard for positions, trades, and performance metrics
What is paper trading mode?

Paper trading is a simulation mode where the bot executes trades with virtual money. It uses real market data (when configured) but doesn't place actual orders on Coinbase. This allows you to:

  • Test your strategy risk-free
  • Learn how the bot works
  • Validate your settings before going live
  • Practice without financial risk

You can enable/disable paper trading in the Settings page under "Trading Mode".

How do I navigate the dashboard?

The dashboard has several key pages:

  • Overview - Main dashboard showing bot status, quick stats, and recent activity
  • Market Conditions - Real-time analysis of why trades are or aren't triggering
  • Positions - View and manage active trading positions
  • Trade History - Complete log of all executed trades
  • Performance - Detailed analytics and charts
  • Portfolio - Portfolio analytics and asset allocation
  • Charts - Technical charts with indicators
  • Settings - Configure trading parameters and preferences
  • Help - This page with FAQs and guides

üìà Trading Strategy

How does the trading bot decide when to trade?

The bot uses a combination of technical indicators:

  • EMA (Exponential Moving Average) - Identifies trend direction
  • RSI (Relative Strength Index) - Measures momentum and overbought/oversold conditions
  • Volume Analysis - Confirms trade signals with volume spikes

For LONG positions:

  • Price must be above EMA(50)
  • RSI must be between 55-70 (bullish but not overbought)
  • Volume must be at least 1.5x the average volume
  • Confidence score must be ‚â•70%

For SHORT positions:

  • Price must be below EMA(50)
  • RSI must be between 30-45 (bearish but not oversold)
  • Volume must be at least 1.5x the average volume
  • Confidence score must be ‚â•70%
What is the confidence score?

The confidence score is a calculated percentage (0-100%) that measures how strongly all trading signals align. It's based on:

  • How well price aligns with EMA trend
  • How optimal the RSI reading is within the entry range
  • How strong the volume confirmation is
  • Overall market conditions

The bot only executes trades when the confidence score meets your minimum threshold (default: 70%). Higher confidence = higher probability of a successful trade, but may result in fewer trades.

How does the bot exit positions?

Positions are closed when any of these conditions are met:

  • Take Profit - Price reaches the target profit level (default: 0.15-0.40%)
  • Stop Loss - Price hits the stop loss level (default: 0.10-0.50%)
  • Time-based - Position is held longer than the timeout period (default: 10 minutes)
  • Signal Reversal - Trading signals reverse, indicating trend change

Take profit and stop loss levels are dynamically calculated based on market volatility and confidence score.

What trading pairs does the bot support?

The bot supports all trading pairs available on Coinbase Advanced Trade. By default, it's configured to trade BTC-USD and ETH-USD, but you can:

  • Add any Coinbase trading pair in the Settings page
  • Click "Refresh List" to see all available pairs
  • Add or remove pairs as needed
  • Trade multiple pairs simultaneously (up to your max positions limit)

üõ°Ô∏è Risk Management

What safety features does the bot have?

The bot includes multiple layers of risk protection:

  • Position Sizing - Each trade risks only a small percentage of your account (default: 0.25%)
  • Daily Loss Limit - Bot stops trading after reaching the daily loss limit (default: $2,000)
  • Maximum Positions - Limits how many positions can be open at once (default: 2)
  • Position Timeout - Forces positions to close after maximum hold time (default: 10 minutes)
  • Maximum Position Size - Prevents overexposure on a single trade (default: 50% of account)
  • Emergency Kill Switch - Instantly stops all trading activity
  • Pre-trade Validation - All trades are validated before execution
How do I set my risk per trade?

Risk per trade is configured in Settings under "Risk Management":

  • Go to Settings ‚Üí Risk Management
  • Find "Risk Per Trade (%)"
  • Enter your desired percentage (default: 0.25%)
  • Click "Save Settings"
  • Click "Apply & Restart Bot" to apply changes

Recommendations:

  • Conservative: 0.1-0.25%
  • Moderate: 0.25-0.5%
  • Aggressive: 0.5-1.0% (not recommended for beginners)

Warning: Higher risk per trade means larger potential losses if a trade goes against you.

What happens when I reach the daily loss limit?

When your daily P&L reaches the configured daily loss limit:

  • The bot automatically stops opening new positions
  • Existing positions remain open until they hit their stop loss or take profit
  • The bot status changes to "Paused"
  • You'll see a notification in the dashboard
  • The bot will resume automatically the next day (loss limit resets daily)

You can manually resume the bot before the next day if needed, but this is not recommended as it defeats the purpose of the safety limit.

How do I use the emergency kill switch?

The kill switch immediately stops all trading activity:

  1. Go to the Overview page
  2. Find the "Emergency Kill Switch" button (red)
  3. Click it and confirm the action

When activated, the kill switch will:

  • Stop the bot immediately
  • Close all open positions (if configured)
  • Prevent new trades from being placed
  • Require manual intervention to restart

Use the kill switch if:

  • You notice unexpected behavior
  • Market conditions are extremely volatile
  • You need to stop trading immediately for any reason

üîß Troubleshooting

Why isn't the bot making any trades?

If the bot isn't trading, check these common issues:

  1. Bot Status - Make sure the bot is "RUNNING" (not stopped or paused)
  2. Market Conditions - Go to "Market Conditions" page to see why trades aren't triggering
  3. Confidence Threshold - Your minimum confidence may be too high (check Settings)
  4. RSI Range - Current market conditions may not meet your RSI entry requirements
  5. Volume - Volume may not be sufficient (needs 1.5x average by default)
  6. Maximum Positions - You may already have the maximum number of positions open
  7. Daily Loss Limit - You may have reached your daily loss limit
  8. Account Balance - Insufficient balance for position sizing

The Market Conditions page shows exactly why trades aren't triggering for each trading pair.

The bot shows "Insufficient Data" for a trading pair

This means the bot doesn't have enough historical candle data to calculate indicators. This happens when:

  • A trading pair was just added
  • The Coinbase API connection is having issues
  • The pair is new or has low trading volume

Solutions:

  • Wait a few minutes for the bot to collect enough data
  • Check your API connection in Settings
  • Try refreshing the market conditions page
  • If using paper trading, ensure "Use Real Market Data" is enabled

The bot typically needs about 50+ candles (50 minutes for 1-minute candles) to start making trading decisions.

I'm getting API errors or connection issues

If you see API errors:

  1. Check API Keys - Verify your Coinbase API keys are correct in Settings
  2. Check Permissions - Ensure your API keys have "View" and "Trade" permissions
  3. Check Rate Limits - Coinbase has rate limits; if exceeded, wait a few minutes
  4. Check Network - Ensure your internet connection is stable
  5. Check Coinbase Status - Visit Coinbase status page to see if there are outages
  6. Paper Trading - If using paper trading mode, API keys are optional

If issues persist, check the Logs page for detailed error messages.

How do I view system logs?

To view logs:

  1. Click "Logs" in the navigation menu
  2. Use the dropdown to filter by log level (INFO, WARNING, ERROR, DEBUG)
  3. Use the search box to find specific log messages
  4. Click "Download Logs" to save a copy

Logs auto-refresh every 5 seconds when you're on the Logs page.

üîå API Integration

Do I need Coinbase API keys?

For Paper Trading: API keys are optional. The bot can work with synthetic data or public market data.

For Live Trading: API keys are required. You'll need:

  • API Key
  • API Secret
  • API Passphrase

Even in paper trading, API keys are recommended because:

  • You get real market prices
  • You can test with actual market conditions
  • You can practice with real trading pairs
How do I get Coinbase API keys?
  1. Log in to your Coinbase account
  2. Go to Settings ‚Üí API
  3. Click "New API Key"
  4. Select permissions:
    • View - Required to see account balance and market data
    • Trade - Required to place orders (only needed for live trading)
  5. Copy your API Key, API Secret, and Passphrase
  6. In the bot's Settings page, enter these credentials
  7. Click "Save Settings"

Security Tips:

  • Never share your API keys
  • Use "View" only permissions for paper trading
  • Enable IP whitelisting if available
  • Rotate keys regularly
What permissions do I need for my API keys?

For Paper Trading:

  • View - Required (to get real market data)
  • Trade permissions are NOT needed

For Live Trading:

  • View - Required
  • Trade - Required (to place orders)

Recommended: Start with "View" only permissions for paper trading. Only enable "Trade" when you're ready for live trading.

‚öôÔ∏è Settings & Configuration

How do I change trading parameters?
  1. Go to the Settings page
  2. Modify any parameter you want to change:
    • Strategy parameters (EMA, RSI, Volume)
    • Risk management settings
    • Exit parameters
    • Trading pairs
    • Trading mode
  3. Click "Save Settings"
  4. Click "Apply & Restart Bot" to restart the bot with new settings

Note: Settings changes require restarting the bot to take effect.

What are configuration templates?

Configuration templates allow you to save and quickly switch between different trading configurations:

  • Save Template - Save your current settings with a name (e.g., "Conservative", "Aggressive", "BTC Only")
  • Load Template - Quickly apply a saved configuration
  • Delete Template - Remove templates you no longer need

Use cases:

  • Test different strategies
  • Switch between conservative and aggressive settings
  • Quickly configure for different market conditions
  • Save proven configurations
How do I reset settings to defaults?
  1. Go to the Settings page
  2. Scroll to the bottom
  3. Click "Reset to Defaults"
  4. Confirm the action
  5. Click "Save Settings"
  6. Click "Apply & Restart Bot" to apply

Default values:

  • EMA Period: 50
  • RSI Period: 14
  • Risk Per Trade: 0.25%
  • Max Positions: 2
  • Daily Loss Limit: $2,000
  • Min Confidence: 70%
