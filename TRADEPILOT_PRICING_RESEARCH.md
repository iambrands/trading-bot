# TradePilot Pricing Model Research & Recommendations

**Date:** December 2025  
**Purpose:** Market and competitor research to determine optimal pricing strategy for TradePilot

---

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive market research, TradePilot should adopt a **hybrid pricing model** combining:
1. **Tiered Subscription** (Primary) - Monthly/Annual recurring revenue
2. **Freemium Tier** (Acquisition) - Free tier to attract users
3. **Performance Fee** (Premium) - Optional profit-sharing for higher tiers
4. **Lifetime License** (One-time) - Capture high-value customers

**Recommended Price Points:**
- **Free:** Limited features, paper trading only
- **Starter:** $29/month ($290/year) - 2 trading pairs, basic features
- **Pro:** $79/month ($790/year) - Unlimited pairs, advanced features
- **Enterprise:** $199/month ($1,990/year) - White-label, API, priority support
- **Lifetime:** $2,997 one-time - Full features, lifetime updates

## Pricing Model Analysis

### 1. Subscription-Based (Most Common)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Predictable recurring revenue (MRR/ARR)
- ✅ Easier customer budgeting
- ✅ Enables continuous product updates
- ✅ Low barrier to entry (monthly payments)
- ✅ Industry standard

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Ongoing costs for support/updates
- ❌ Churn risk
- ❌ May deter price-sensitive users
- ❌ Requires continuous value delivery

**Best For:** Primary revenue model, stable long-term growth

### 2. Freemium Model

**Advantages:**
- ✅ High user acquisition rate
- ✅ Low customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- ✅ Natural upgrade path
- ✅ Viral growth potential
- ✅ Market education/lead generation

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Free users consume resources
- ❌ Lower conversion rates (1-5% typical)
- ❌ May devalue premium offerings
- ❌ Support costs for free users

**Best For:** User acquisition, market penetration, education-focused products

**TradePilot Fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Perfect - Educational focus aligns with freemium model

### 3. Performance-Based / Revenue Share

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Aligns pricing with customer success
- ✅ "Win-win" positioning
- ✅ Appeals to performance-focused traders
- ✅ Higher price tolerance for profitable users

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Revenue unpredictability
- ❌ Complex accounting/tracking
- ❌ May deter risk-averse users
- ❌ Requires transparency/trust

**Common Structures:**
- 10-20% of profits (capped)
- 5-15% of account growth
- Monthly fee + percentage of gains

**Best For:** Premium tier, performance-focused traders, differentiation

**TradePilot Fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good - Could differentiate in premium tiers

### 4. One-Time / Lifetime License

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Large upfront revenue
- ✅ No churn concerns
- ✅ Appeals to price-conscious long-term users
- ✅ Predictable for user (no recurring fees)

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Revenue concentration risk
- ❌ Lifetime support costs
- ❌ Difficult to fund ongoing development
- ❌ Limited scalability

**Best For:** Special offers, high-value customers, market penetration

**TradePilot Fit:** ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate - Good for special launches or high-value customers

### 5. Usage-Based / Transaction Fees

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Scales with usage
- ✅ Fair for low-volume users
- ✅ Aligns costs with value

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Revenue unpredictability
- ❌ May discourage usage
- ❌ Complex billing
- ❌ Less common in trading bots

**Best For:** Enterprise/API tiers, high-volume users

**TradePilot Fit:** ⭐⭐ Limited - Could work for API/enterprise tier

## Alternative Pricing Models to Consider

### Option A: Performance-Based Hybrid

**Structure:** Base subscription + performance fee

- **Free:** Paper trading only
- **Starter:** $29/month + 5% of monthly profits (capped at $500/month)
- **Pro:** $59/month + 3% of monthly profits (capped at $1,000/month)
- **Enterprise:** $149/month + 2% of monthly profits (uncapped)

**Advantages:**
- Aligns with customer success
- Lower base price barrier
- Higher revenue potential from successful traders

**Disadvantages:**
- Revenue unpredictability
- Complex tracking/accounting
- May deter risk-averse users

**Best For:** Differentiation, appeal to performance-focused traders

### Option B: Per-Strategy Pricing

**Structure:** Base tier + strategy add-ons

- **Free:** 1 strategy (EMA+RSI+Volume only)
- **Starter:** $29/month - 2 strategies
- **Pro:** $79/month - 5 strategies
- **Enterprise:** $199/month - Unlimited strategies

**Advantages:**
- Scalable pricing
- Clear value proposition
- Easy to understand

**Disadvantages:**
- Limits current offering (TradePilot has 1 primary strategy)
- May confuse users
- Not aligned with current feature set

**Best For:** Future expansion when multiple strategies are available

### Option C: Transaction-Based

**Structure:** Free base + per-trade fees

- **Free:** Paper trading unlimited
- **Live Trading:** $0.10 per trade or 0.1% of trade value (whichever is higher)
- **Subscription options:** Monthly cap on fees

**Advantages:**
- Fair for low-volume users
- Scales with usage
- Aligns with value

**Disadvantages:**
- Revenue unpredictability
- May discourage active trading
- Complex billing

**Best For:** If targeting high-frequency traders exclusively
